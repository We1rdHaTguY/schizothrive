ChatGPT: Simple Ten Dollar
Vegetable Soup Recipe


I decided to harness ChatGPT today to help me come up with a simple and easy vegetable soup recipe that I can make using mostly canned goods. I'm no chef, so ChatGPT and other AI chat bots are of great use to me for coming up with proper ingredients and portions. I’ve been looking for a simple way to incorporate more veggies into my current diet that aren’t raw because I’ve done the raw vegan thing a long time ago and it made me feel great at the time except for one thing; it gave me serious constipation. My current diet isn’t a vegan diet, but I’m straying away from carb-loaded foods like baked goods, pastries, pasta, sugary sweets, in favor of whole food options, and I try to moderate my intake of meat enough that I’m not solely relying on meat as a replacement for carb-loaded options. I also wanted to increase my intake of important nutrients and felt like a veggie soup would be the perfect fit as something tasty that will mostly satisfy those needs.

Below is the recipe I came up with for myself with the help of ChatGPT that will make about 4-6 servings (depending on whether you’re having it as a main dish or a side dish, I like to store it away for another 3-4 reheatable dishes throughout the week) for around $10: 

Ingredients:

  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can mixed vegetables (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 can corn (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 can green beans (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 can potatoes (diced, drained) or 1 cup frozen diced potatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning (or a mix of thyme, oregano, and basil)
Optional Ingredients:
  • 1–2 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes
  • Spinach

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onion and garlic:
    In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic and cook for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add Canned Veggies & tomatoes:
    Dump in the diced tomatoes (with juice) and all veggies.
  3. Add broth and seasoning:
    Pour in the broth and stir in your spices. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Simmer:
    Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes to let flavors meld. Stir occasionally. Add in spinach about 5 minutes before finishing.

In my first batch I’m going to use 1 tbsp of butter, a pinch of red pepper, and spinach as my optional ingredients. I won’t be using onion or garlic, so I can skip the first step, making it about a 15-20 minute meal to cook. I’m also going to use raw potatoes instead of canned and I’ll be using vegetable cooking stock instead of broth only because where I shop has a limited selection. I’m cooking this up only for myself though, so I won’t just get a single meal out of it, I’ll get another 3-5 meals I can store away and reheat on the stove or in the microwave. For roughly $10, that’s really not a bad deal at all! (Especially in this economy). I’ll share my results and whether or not I’ll be making tweaks to the recipe in another post. Thanks for comin’!
